# Mallorca Website Brief and Execution Plan

Date: 2026-03-27

- Primary issue: [MAL-7](/MAL/issues/MAL-7)
- Supports: [MAL-3](/MAL/issues/MAL-3), [MAL-6](/MAL/issues/MAL-6)

## Objective

Launch a focused front-facing website that explains the Mallorca market-intelligence product, speaks to the first buyer, and gives prospects a clear next action.

The site should behave like a B2B landing page for an acquisition-radar product, not like a consumer property portal.

## Audience

- Boutique buyer's agents serving international premium-resale buyers in Mallorca.
- Small acquisition teams sourcing inventory in Palma, Calvia, and Andratx.

## Core Message

- Track premium-resale supply in Mallorca before the market moves past you.
- Replace manual portal checks and scattered broker tabs with one evidence-backed market-intelligence workflow.
- See new listings, price cuts, duplicates, and neighborhood comps in one place.

## Required Story On The Page

1. Hero: who the product is for, what it does, and one clear CTA.
2. Problem: fragmented portals, delayed awareness, duplicate listings, and manual comps work.
3. Solution: acquisition radar for Palma and the southwest corridor with source-backed evidence.
4. Product proof: show the current intelligence surface around supply, pricing, and comps.
5. Workflow: how a buyer's agent or acquisition team uses the product each week.
6. CTA: request access, book a demo, or join the waitlist.

## Proof Points To Emphasize

- Mallorca-specific focus, starting with Palma plus the southwest corridor.
- Coverage model built around listing changes, price moves, duplicate collapse, and neighborhood comps.
- Auditable source evidence for each record and change path.
- Existing internal product slice already produces structured supply, pricing, and comparable-market outputs.

## Design Direction

- Premium, serious, and operator-first.
- Avoid generic proptech marketplace visuals and consumer-search metaphors.
- Use maps, listing-change examples, and market snapshots as visual anchors.
- Keep the first launch narrow: one language, one CTA path, one audience.

## Implementation Notes

- Build the website inside the active Mallorca project workspace so product and marketing stay in one delivery path.
- Reuse the current TypeScript stack unless a materially better landing-page path is justified.
- Start with a single launch page plus basic contact capture or waitlist flow.
- Add lightweight placeholders for future case studies or market reports, but do not expand scope into a CMS in the first cut.

## Execution Breakdown

- CEO: lock positioning, page structure, and acceptance criteria.
- Analyst: supply source-backed proof points, market examples, and any claims that need validation.
- Founding Engineer: implement the landing page and ship the initial deployable website slice.

## Definition Of Done

- A public-facing page exists in the Mallorca project and reflects the first buyer and wedge.
- The page clearly explains the problem, solution, and CTA in terms aligned with [MAL-3](/MAL/issues/MAL-3).
- The page includes real product or market proof, not placeholder marketing copy alone.
- The implementation is ready for the next step toward hosting or distribution.
